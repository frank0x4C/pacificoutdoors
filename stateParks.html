Washington State Parks

Every year, almost 40 million people visit Washington’s 124 state parks. With over 400 miles of hiking trails and locations in every corner of the state — from Peace Arch Historical State Park in the northwest to Fort Columbia Historical State Park in the southwest, to Crawford State Park Heritage Site in the northeast and Fields Spring State Park in the southeast — these parks provide unique recreation activities for all. Washington is lucky — at 106 years old, our state park system is one of the oldest in the country. Older, in fact, than the National Park system. And though the state is similar in size to Utah, we have just about the triple the state parks. And forget your typical definition of park. Ours are diverse in both in type (cultural, recreational, historical, geological) and variety (marine parks, ocean front, steppe shrub, mountainous, old growth forest to name a few).

Pass: Discover Pass($30/year) or pay daily entry fee ($10/day)
